Common Questions

Concrete & Paving FAQ

Answers to what Omaha homeowners and businesses ask us most.

How much does a concrete driveway cost in Omaha?

Concrete driveway costs vary by size, thickness, site prep (removal of an old driveway, grading, drainage), and finish (broom finish vs. stamped/decorative). A free on-site estimate is the most accurate way to price your specific project, since soil conditions and access can change the cost as much as square footage does.

How long does a typical concrete or paving project take?

A standard residential driveway or patio pour usually takes 1–3 days for the pour and finishing, plus a curing period of about a week before it can handle vehicle weight (concrete continues to gain strength for about 28 days). Larger commercial paving or parking lot projects are scoped and scheduled individually based on square footage and phasing needs.

Can concrete be poured in the winter in Nebraska?

Yes, with the right precautions. Cold-weather concrete work requires ground thawing, insulated blankets or heated enclosures, and a mix design adjusted for lower temperatures so the concrete can cure properly before it freezes. Winter pours generally need more lead time for scheduling and weather monitoring than a summer project.

Do you offer free estimates?

Yes. Reddy Steady Go LLC provides free, written, on-site estimates for concrete, masonry, paving, and repair projects across the Omaha metro. Call (402) 415-9253 or use the contact form to request one.

What's the difference between mudjacking and full concrete replacement?

Mudjacking (slab leveling) lifts and levels an existing sunken concrete slab by pumping material underneath it, which is faster and less expensive than a full tear-out. It works well when the slab itself is still structurally sound and just needs leveling. If the concrete is badly cracked, crumbling, or the sub-base has failed, full replacement is usually the better long-term fix.
